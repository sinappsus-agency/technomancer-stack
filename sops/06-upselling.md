# SOP 06 — Upselling and Cross-Selling

**Category:** Upselling + Cross-Selling
**Version:** 1.0
**Owner:** Studio Lead
**Trigger:** Project at 70% completion, OR retainer renewal within 30 days, OR major deliverable just accepted
**Frequency:** Per eligible engagement

---

## Objective

Create natural, non-coercive pathways for clients to expand their engagement — as the next logical step in their progress, not as a sales push.

The best upsell offers itself because the client is experiencing the problem the next service solves.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Current engagement is delivering well (on time, within scope, positive feedback received)
- [ ] You understand the client's next constraint or goal
- [ ] Client context.md is current

---

## The Service Expansion Map

Before the upsell conversation, map the expansion logic:

```
What they bought → What they need next → Why it follows naturally
```

Common progressions in a creative/tech studio:

| Current engagement | Natural next | Logic |
|---|---|---|
| Brand identity only | Website or landing page | "You now have a brand — let's put it to work" |
| Website | Email marketing setup | "You have traffic — let's capture it" |
| One-off project | Retainer | "You keep needing changes — let's systemize" |
| Retainer | Strategy consulting | "Results are improving — let's scale the thinking" |
| Any | SOP / systems product | "You're growing — let's make it repeatable" |
| Any | Training your team | "You want to own this internally — let's make you capable" |

---

## Steps

**Step 1 — Identify the expansion signal**
Watch for these signals in client interactions:
- "We're thinking about doing [X] next..."
- "Is that something you could help with?"
- "I wish we had [Y] sorted"
- You notice a gap that is costing them (a broken process, a missing tool, a weak channel)

Log the signal in context.md under "expansion opportunities."

**Step 2 — Validate before pitching**
Ask a discovery question before proposing anything:
*"You mentioned [X] — is that something on your radar for this quarter? I have a thought on how to approach it."*

This tests buying intent without committing you to a proposal. If they say "yes, definitely" → proceed. If "not right now" → log it with a follow-up date.

**Step 3 — Frame the expansion, not the service**
When raising the idea formally, lead with their outcome, not your service:

❌ Bad: *"We offer a retainer package that covers ongoing updates..."*
✅ Good: *"Based on what we've been doing, the next thing that would make the biggest difference is [outcome]. Want me to sketch out what that would look like?"*

**Step 4 — Send an expansion proposal**
If they want to hear more, send a short proposal (not a full deck):

```
Subject: [CLIENT NAME] — Proposed next step

Hi [NAME],

Based on our work together and what you mentioned about [goal/problem], here is what I'd suggest next:

PROPOSED SCOPE:
[2–3 sentences describing exactly what you'd do]

WHAT YOU GET:
- [Outcome 1]
- [Outcome 2]

INVESTMENT:
[Price] / [Retainer rate] for [duration]

Would a 20-minute call this week to talk it through work for you?

[Name]
```

**Step 5 — Wait for a response — once**
After sending the proposal, wait 3–5 business days. If no response, send one follow-up:
*"Just pinging this back to the top of your inbox — happy to talk through any questions."*
Then let it rest. Do not follow up more than twice on an upsell.

---

## Cross-Selling

Cross-selling applies when the client has a need that falls into an adjacent service category you offer.

Rules:
1. Only raise a cross-sell if you are genuinely confident it is relevant to their current situation
2. Never raise a cross-sell while the current project has unresolved issues
3. Make it sound like an observation, not a pitch: *"I noticed [X] while working on your [Y]..."*

---

## Checkpoints

- Do not raise an upsell during a project that is behind schedule or has open quality issues.
- Do not raise more than one expansion idea at a time.
- Log all expansion opportunities and dispositions in context.md regardless of outcome.

## Failure Modes and Escalation

**Failure: Client says no to the upsell**
This is normal. Log the response, the reason if given, and a future date to revisit (6 months out). Never treat a "no" as a relationship problem — treat it as a timing signal.

**Failure: Client accepts but budget is smaller than your ask**
Have a tiered version of your offer ready. Know in advance where your floor is and what a reduced-scope version looks like.

**Failure: You raise the upsell too early**
If the client's response is cool, it may be timing. Note it, back off, and let the work speak louder. The next checkpoint is the 30-day follow-up (SOP 02).
